Introduction:

Tinsmithy encompasses the study of working with thin metal sheets, including techniques for cutting, shaping, bending, and joining metals like tin, aluminum, or light alloys to create various products such as containers, roofing materials, and decorative items. It also involves understanding metal properties, safety measures, and the use of specialized tools and equipment in the tinsmithing process.

EXP: 01 RECTANGULAR TRAY AIM: To make a rectangular Tray as per required dimensions. 110 ASUS VivoBook S Engineering Work Shop GR22 MATERIALS REQUIRED: Galvanized Iron sheet (150*110*26 gauge) TOOLS AND EQUIPMENT REQUIRED: 1. Steel rule 2. Flat file 3. Scriber 4. Try square 5. Snips 6. Dot punch 7. Stakes 8. Mallet OPERATIONS TO BE CARRIED OUT: 1. Planning 2. Marking 3. Cutting 4. Bending 5. Seaming 6. Soldering PROCEDURE: 1. The size of the given sheet is checked with steel rule. 2. Mark the measurement and make the development surface sketch diagram. 3. The layout of the tray is marked on given sheet. 4. The layout of the tray is cut by using the straight snips. 5. The sheet is bent to the required shape using stakes and mallet. 6. Now the bent edges are made to overlap each other and stuck with a mallet to get the required joint. 7. The joint is soldered. PRECAUTIONS: 1 . Care should be taken while cutting with snip. 2. Care should be taken while bending and hemming. RESULT: The rectangular tray is done successfully made EXPERIMENT 2: OPEN SCOOP AIM: To make a open scoop from the given sheet metal. MATERIAL SUPPLIED: 30-gauge galvanized iron (GI) sheet 110 x 120mm TOOLS REQUIRED: 1. Steel rule 2. Mallet 3. Scriber 4. Divider 5. Protractor 6. Straight snip SEQUENCE OF OPERATIONS: 1. Checking 2. Levelling 3. Marking 4. Cutting 5. Bending 6. Seaming PROCEDURE: 1. The size of the given sheet is checked with the steel rule. 2. The layout of the scoop is marked on the given sheet. 3. The layout of the scoop is cut by using the straight snip. 4. The corners of the scoop are hemmed. 5. The corners of the scoop can be riveted or soldered to ensure stability of the joints. PRECAUTIONS: 1. Mark the dimensions correctly 2. Cutting of the sheet should be done carefully. 3. Clean the chips with brush. RESULT: The desired open scoop is obtained.
